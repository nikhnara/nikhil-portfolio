Journey

A story about learning by building: from early data engineering roots to applied data science, startup execution, and now leading data strategy for a city department.

  1. Data Engineering Foundations

    I started on the engineering side of data: modeling sources, building pipelines, and learning why reliability and documentation matter as much as models.

  2. M.S. in Applied Data Science, Indiana University

    Deepened my focus on machine learning with a concentration in deep learning and neural networks. Coursework and projects pushed me to think in systems: data quality in, useful predictions out.

  3. Graduate Research Assistant (Data Analyst)

    Put theory into practice: exploratory analysis, forecasting, and dashboarding to support academic programs. Learned to translate ambiguous questions into measurable metrics and clear visuals.

  4. Data Analyst, Konnex AI (Bloomington Startup)

    Joined as an early team member and worked directly with client SeaPro. Leveraged ML and statistical methods to improve business decisions. Beyond hands-on analytics, I shaped product analytics, pitched ideas to new clients, and owned delivery end to end.

  5. Senior Data Analyst, Louisville Metro Parks & Recreation

    Moved into a senior role to lead data strategy across the department. Partner with finance and marketing to build a durable data culture: standard models, shared metrics, and decision-ready dashboards for 50+ sites. Recently began guiding the team into AI use cases, starting with practical chatbots.

  6. Personal Interests and Exploration

    I keep a builder's habit: NLP experiments, RAG pipelines, and comparing OpenAI and Gemini features to find cost-effective, reliable patterns. I evaluate not just model quality but also operational cost and maintainability.

Through each step, the common thread is ownership: ship value, explain simply, and leave teams stronger than I found them.
